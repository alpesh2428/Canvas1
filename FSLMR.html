What is the purpose of the FSLMR Statute?
To define federal employee labor rights and promote effective labor-management relations.
What rights do employees have under §7102?
To join or refrain from labor organizations and engage in collective bargaining.
Who is excluded from the definition of "employee" under §7103?
Supervisors, management officials, uniformed services, strikers, and others.
What is the FLRA?
Federal Labor Relations Authority – oversees federal labor-management relations.
What is a "grievance" under the statute?
Any complaint by an employee, union, or agency over employment-related matters.
Key management rights under §7106?
Hire, assign, direct, discipline, lay off employees, and manage agency operations.
What is an "unfair labor practice" for agencies?
Actions like coercing employees or refusing to bargain in good faith (§7116).
What is the role of the Federal Service Impasses Panel?
Resolves negotiation impasses between agencies and unions (§7119).
When does a labor org get exclusive recognition?
After being elected by majority vote in a secret ballot (§7111).
What is required for dues deduction?
A signed employee authorization; valid for 1 year (§7115).
